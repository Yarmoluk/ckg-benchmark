\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented the CKG Benchmark, the first large-scale comparison of Compact
Knowledge Graphs against RAG across 44 educational domains (7,588 CKG queries,
3,499 RAG queries). Our key findings are:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{CKG achieves 4$\times$ higher macro F1 than RAG} on structural
    knowledge queries (0.45 vs.\ 0.11), using 11$\times$ fewer tokens per query
    (274 vs.\ 3,018). The compound RDS advantage is 44.4$\times$.
  \item \textbf{Structural query performance is near-ceiling for aggregation}:
    T4 category aggregates reach F1\,$=$\,0.95 for CKG vs.\ 0.22 for RAG,
    because the answer is fully determined by the taxonomy column of the CSV.
  \item \textbf{CKG F1 increases with hop depth} (peaking at hop=3: 0.64)
    while RAG degrades, confirming that deterministic edge traversal does not
    suffer the multi-hop retrieval recall failures inherent to passage-based
    systems.
  \item \textbf{T1 (entity lookup) is a documented negative control}:
    CKG scores 0.19 on explanatory queries, confirming the benchmark is not
    constructed to favour CKG universally. Both systems struggle on T1, which
    requires prose definitions absent from DAG structure.
  \item \textbf{RDS and Hop-Depth F1} are practical additions to IR evaluation
    that jointly measure quality and efficiency---metrics missing from existing
    benchmarks such as BEIR and RAGAS.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Limitations.}
RAG results cover 21 of 44 domains due to corpus availability; GraphRAG
evaluation is pending. Ground truth for T2--T4 is derived from the same DAG
used by CKG, which is a methodological constraint acknowledged in the design
(Section~\ref{sec:benchmark}). T1 performance on explanatory queries is weak
for both systems and represents an open problem.

\paragraph{Future Work.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Complete GraphRAG evaluation to enable three-way comparison.
  \item Extend T5 cross-concept retrieval with deeper BFS path-finding; current
    T5 F1 of 0.33 has a clear improvement trajectory.
  \item Hybrid architectures combining CKG structural precision with RAG's
    prose retrieval for T1/T5 query types.
  \item Extend beyond educational domains to legal, medical, and financial
    knowledge graphs.
  \item Automated CKG construction from unstructured text, eliminating the
    expert curation requirement.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Open Benchmark.}
The complete benchmark---corpus, queries, evaluation harness, and all
results---is released at
\url{https://huggingface.co/datasets/graphify-md/ckg-benchmark}
under CC BY 4.0 (data) and MIT (code). We invite the community to add
domains, systems, and metrics.
