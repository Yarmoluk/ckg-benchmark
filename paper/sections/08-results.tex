\subsection{F1 by Hop Depth}

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\caption{CKG F1 by hop depth. Higher hop depth = deeper prerequisite chains.}
\label{tab:hop-depth}
\begin{tabular}{lcccccc}
\toprule
\textbf{System} & \textbf{hop=0} & \textbf{hop=1} & \textbf{hop=2} & \textbf{hop=3} & \textbf{hop=4} & \textbf{hop=5} \\
\midrule
CKG & 0.3409 & 0.4814 & 0.6191 & 0.6445 & 0.6118 & 0.5933 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\noindent CKG F1 \textit{increases} with hop depth up to hop=3, then plateaus.
This is the inverse of the degradation pattern typically seen in RAG systems,
where multi-hop queries scatter evidence across documents and retrieval recall
falls. CKG traverses edges deterministically, so deeper chains do not degrade
performance.
